My Story

I received my bachelor’s degree in Civil Engineering with a focus on geospatial engineering from the University of Tehran in 2015. During this period, I developed a strong foundation in spatial analysis, remote sensing, and engineering based problem solving. I then completed my master’s degree in Civil Engineering with a specialization in Water Resources Management at Tarbiat Modares University in 2018. My master’s thesis focused on satellite based detection and prediction of algal blooms using machine learning methods, where I combined Earth observation data with data driven modeling to address water quality challenges.

In 2022, I joined Chapman University as a PhD student in Computational and Data Science. My doctoral research centers on GeoAI driven analysis of climate variability, hydrology, and agricultural systems, with an emphasis on integrating remote sensing, climate data, and advanced machine learning and deep learning models. My work includes large scale modeling of agricultural and water systems, assessment of climate extremes, and development of explainable and generalizable data driven frameworks for environmental decision making.

Alongside my primary research in Earth and environmental systems, I have also worked on applied machine learning projects in healthcare. These include the use of machine learning and large language models for lung cancer analysis, prognosis modeling, and clinical data integration. This cross domain experience has strengthened my ability to translate advanced AI methods across disciplines while maintaining a strong grounding in domain knowledge and real world impact.
